Still Alive Trailer

The Game Concept

In “Still Alive”, you are a survivor. One of the few who survived in a world that has been hit by meteorites 25 years ago leaving the world in a devastating apocalypse. Extraterrestrial microorganisms have taken over the world, keeping humans in constant battle for survival. You are one of them. You are still alive.

The battle is on, for each player, or two teams.

Competitive online multiplayer at its finest. In a deathmatch-like game your primary goal is to stay alive. You have to fight your way through hordes of enemies while searching for an antidote to protect yourself against a poisonous atmosphere. Since there are never enough antidotes for each player in the match, you have to compete against others to get a hold on these vital injections.

The game can be enjoyed with up to 10 players fighting against each other (Standard Mode) or form two teams of up to 5 players that join forces against the other team (Team Survival Mode).

1954 Alcatraz

My Role:

  • 2D Artist
  • UI Designer

1954 Alcatraz

This was my first project in the Game Industry. I joined the Team just when they started polishing the Prototype.

I cleaned a lot of the Background Illustrations and prepared them for implementing into the Engine. It was a challenge to adjust a 2D Background image for a 3D Character.

I also concepted the UI and created and prepared the Assets for it.

I really loved working with the team and it felt like a great start diving into creating Games.

Tools I Used:

Talk Menu and Cursor

The Talk Menu and Cursor on a Point and Click Adventure is important, because you basically do two things in Games like this, you pickup and combine Items - and you talk.

Usually there are always two actions a player can do and UX wise its smart to offer both options on the left and right mouse button. Having two actions I quickly thought of having one hand for one and another. Handcuffs, the curser had to be handcuffs, because that just fits with a prison setted Game.

The Talkmenu just had to get a prison theme too.

For the second playable Character the talkmenu should change into something more sophisticated, as the character was not in prison.

Lastly I planed the Inventory to be Police Files, whith Portrait photos to switch the playable Character.

Game Menu

I enjoyed working close with the developper on this one. I had the Idea, that when the Player wants to enter the Game Menu Prisonbars slide in with a nice atmosphearic soundeffect. And for each Menupoint the attachement on the bars would change quickly with a slide out and back in again.
